Quartic GroundControl

All your data managed, prepared, and analysed in one platform. Exchange real time updates with the field.

 

Knowledge base

GroundControl’s knowledge base collates data from across your enterprise to build a seamless picture of your assets, personnel, and operations.

Data integration
Data sources are monitored continuously and combined intelligently to give a unified, searchable view of all of your data.

Situational awareness
Map and dashboard views give control room staff a bird's eye view of operations as they happen. When things go wrong, anyone in the organisation can dig in deeper to help diagnose issues.

Documentations and processes
are stored and kept up-to-date, published instantly to personnel in the field.

Artificial intelligence

GroundControl's machine learning algorithms sift through the data in the knowledge base, picking out trends and either triggering actions directly, or flagging for further investigation by a human.

Telemetry
Where telemetry data is available for assets, this can be monitored in realtime to spot potential failure conditions or simply surface unusual situations.

Inspection reports, work orders and asset records
are processed by online learning algorithms to identify repeated patterns of failure or inefficiencies in field operations. Simple visualisations enable humans to quickly investigate these issues and update the knowledge base appropriately.

Automatic scheduling
Whilst human action may be required in some cases, Quartic can automatically schedule work in cases where this is desired. This puts the right person in the right place at the right time.

Mobile app

The GroundControl mobile app takes the enterprise to the field. Your engineers get the information they need at all times, and can reliably capture knowledge back in a paperless world.

Unified context at a touch
Once an engineer has been scheduled, they’re presented with context-dependent information from the knowledge base - documentation, historical maintenance trends, process changes, and more.

Manual data collection
The paperless office is also taking over the field. Data about faults, replaced parts, and job times can be used to inform future predictions and maintenance schedules.

Offline operations
Engineers often work in difficult locations without mobile reception, so the Quartic app stores as much as possible, and automatically pushes back when reception is re-established.
