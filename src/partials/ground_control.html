Quartic GroundControl.

Monitor assets, predict failures, improve processes.

 

Knowledge base

GroundControl’s knowledge base collates data from across your enterprise to build a seamless picture of your assets, personnel, and operations.

Data integration
Data sources are continuously monitored and intelligently combined to give a unified, searchable view of your data.

Situational awareness
The Map and Dashboard give control room staff a bird's eye view of operations as they happen. When things go wrong, anyone in the organisation can dig in deeper to help remediate issues.

Documentation and processes
GroundControl stores documentation, work logs, and processes for your entire field workforce.

Artificial intelligence

GroundControl's machine learning algorithms sift through the knowledge base, identifying trends and early signs of failure. These predictions are used to trigger actions directly, or to notify a human for further investigation.

Telemetry
Telemetry data can be monitored in real time to spot potential failure conditions or simply surface unusual situations.

Inspection reports, work orders and asset records
Field reports are algorithmically processed to identify patterns of failure or inefficiencies in field operations. Visualisations enable users to investigate these issues and update the knowledge base appropriately.

Automatic scheduling
Although human intervention may be required in some cases, Quartic can automatically schedule work as desired. This puts the right person in the right place at the right time.

Mobile app

The GroundControl mobile app takes the enterprise to the field. Your engineers are given the information they need for the job, and can reliably update the knowledge base. So they can leave their notebooks and manuals at home.

Unified context at a touch
Once an engineer has been scheduled, they’re presented with relevant information from the knowledge base - documentation, historical maintenance trends, process changes, and more.

Manual data collection
The paperless office is also taking over the field. Data about faults, replaced parts, and job times are fed back to the knowledge base, to inform future predictions and maintenance schedules.

Offline operations
Engineers often work in difficult locations without mobile reception, so the Quartic app stores as much as possible, and automatically pushes back when reception is available again.
